VIX & Index Sentiment

VIX & Market Fear Gauge: The VIX measures expected market volatility. VIX below 15 indicates calm markets (bullish), 15-25 is normal, above 25 signals fear and uncertainty (bearish). The index sentiment cards show SPY, QQQ, and DIA momentum. Inverse ETFs (SQQQ, SPXU, SDOW) move opposite to indices - use them to confirm bearish trends or as hedge indicators.

Inverse ETF Signal

Inverse ETFs (SQQQ, SPXU, SDOW) move opposite to their benchmark indices. When these show bullish signals while indices show bearish, it confirms downward momentum. Use as a hedge indicator or to validate trend reversals.

Commodities & Crypto

Commodities typically move inverse to USD strength. When DXY rises, gold/silver often fall as they're priced in dollars. Crypto correlates loosely with risk-on sentiment.

Commodities & Crypto Correlation: Gold and silver typically move inverse to the US Dollar - when DXY (Dollar Index) rises, precious metals often fall since they're priced in dollars. Click tabs to switch between Gold (GC=F), Silver (SI=F), Ethereum, and Bitcoin. Crypto often correlates with risk-on sentiment - rising when stocks rally, falling during fear.

Options Analysis

Analyze options trading opportunities with fair value pricing and Greeks.

How to Use: Enter a stock ticker and date range to analyze all options expiring within 90 days. The tool calculates fair values using Black-Scholes and Binomial Tree models, then compares them to market prices. UNDERPRICED options trade below fair value (potential buy), OVERPRICED trade above fair value (potential sell or short), and FAIR are close to theoretical value. Click + Watch to add the ticker to your intraday watchlist.
